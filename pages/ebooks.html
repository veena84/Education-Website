    • Top Finance Institutes include GST as a new syllabi - June 29, 2017 05:54 AM PDT


    The new mode of taxation, GST has already made into the syllabus of accountancy and finance courses at many esteemed institutions.

    While the whole country is waiting for switching over to GST, the Institute of Cost Accountants of India and the Indian Institute of Management, Calcutta is already framing modules to include GST in their syllabus. The Institute of Chartered Accountants of India has already included GST in the exam syllabus of November 2017, reports TOI

    Since Independence, GST is the biggest financial reform the country has seen. The Institutes believe that students need to be fully prepared to handle the analytical issues and calculations that the companies will have to go through in the coming months.

    The Institute of Cost Accountants, is preparing ahead to initiate students to GST. Manash Kumar Thakur, president of the institute, feels that students appearing for the intermediate exam will have to go through an intensive 10-day course on GST in the areas of filing and data entry. Those appearing for the final exam will have to undergo a similar 10-day class in the areas of appeal, assessment and valuation. After finals, successful students will be taken through an advanced course of 120 hours,

    The Vice Chairman of Indirect taxation at the Institute of Chartered Accountants of India, Sushil Goyel, said that their institution has already taken the step and drawn up the course modules for the concept, calculations, conversions, and the overview of GST. Every student will have to take the exam. There will be a 10-mark question on GST in the exam in November that will be compulsory for every student to attempt.
